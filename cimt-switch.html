Pediatric Therapeutic Device

CIMT Switch Device for Children with Cerebral Palsy

An unconstrained wearable therapeutic switch system designed to encourage and track non-dominant hand usage during Constraint-Induced Movement Therapy (CIMT).

Hall Effect Sensor Wireless Communication SolidWorks CAD Pediatric Ergonomics

Key Engineering Contributions

Non-Contact Sensing

Utilized Hall effect sensing mechanisms to create a friction-free, non-constraining switch triggering mechanism for young patients.

Wireless Integration

Embedded wireless communication protocols to log activity and interact with therapeutic feedback systems remotely.

Child-Friendly Form Factor

Iterated lightweight housing enclosures prioritizing pediatric comfort, safety, and non-restrictive joint movement.
